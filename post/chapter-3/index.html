PROJECTS AND RESEARCH

Translating and Validating General Risk Propensity Scale

May 20, 2019

Researchers interested in risk propensity traditionally considered risk taking as a domain-specific phenomenon. However, there is an evidence suggesting that risk taking could be considered as a general trait which could be measured by diffent techniques, among which are self-report questionnaires. Zhang and colleagues (2018) developed a short questionnaire for measuring general risk propensity. In this small project, the goal was to translate the scale into Serbian language, check its reliability, and explore its structural validity.

What is Risk Propensity?

Note: The details will soon be available in an online notebook with code!

Risk taking is surely a phenomenon which occupied research and clinical psyschologists for a long time. However, even they cannot agree on its definition and how it can be measured. One line of researchers suggest that risk taking is domain-specific phenomenon, which esentially means that people will take risk in some domains (e.g. sports) but not in others (e.g. healthcare). Another line of researchers assume that there must be a general trait which drives risk taking in specific domains. Their assumptions often rely on different research which indicate that there is a situational cross-consistency when it comes to taking risks.

Zhang and colleagues (2018) decided to develop a self-report questionnaire which would measure general risk propensity. Having such methodological tool is useful for overcoming bad practices in measuring general risk propensity (e.g. measuring risk taking in different domains and summing up the results). Their scale consists of following items:

  1. Taking risks makes life more fun.
  2. My friends would say that I’m a risk taker.
  3. I enjoy taking risks in most aspects of my life.
  4. I would take a risk even if it meant I might get hurt.
  5. Taking risks is an important part of my life.
  6. I commonly make risky decisions.
  7. I am a believer of taking chances.
  8. I am attracted, rather than scared, by risk.

For each item, participant would have to respond whether they agree or not on a 5-point response scale ranging from 1 - disagree to 5 - agree.

Goal and Project Results

As a part of a bigger project, my colleague Ivana Jovanović and I decided to translate this scale into Serbian language, check its reliability and structure and decide whether the translation could be used for future work. A total of 100 participants participated in research, which gave us information not only on their risk propensity, but on their gender and age. Therefore, we could test the relationship between these features as well.

The results indicated high reliability (0.87) and that structure is comprised of one factor, which in essence is a general risk propensity. Furthermore, age and gender were not in a significant relationship with risk propensity.

Translated Items

  1. Preuzimanje rizika čini život zabavnijim.
  2. Prijatelji bi za mene rekli da sam osoba koja je spremna da preuzme rizik.
  3. Uživam da preuzimam rizik u mnogim aspektima mog života.
  4. Preuzeo/la bih rizik čak i ako bi to značilo da bih mogao/la biti povređen/a.
  5. Preuzimanje rizika je bitan deo mog života.
  6. Često donosim rizične odluke.
  7. ja sam osoba koja veruje da treba rizikovati.
  8. Rizik me više privlači nego što me plaši.
