May 29, 2020

Predicting Employee Turnover

Since employee turnover poses a great issue for companies and organizations regardless of their size, the goal of this project was to develop, evaluate and choose the best model for predicting whether employee will leave the company or not. The goal was accomplished by training several different estimators, tuning hyperparameters and developing more complex ensemble models. The final model achieved an accuracy of 99% on test data! Conclusions and implications are discussed.

Replicating Patterns of Prospect Theory for Decision Under Risk

Co-authored the paper Replicating patterns of prospect theory for decision under risk published in Nature Human Behaviour. The topic of research was highly influential theory of decision making and it was conducted in 19 different countries with more than 4000 subjects! July 31, 2019

Testing the Explanation for Phonological Similarity Effect

Phonological similarity effect refers to an observation that the recall of phonologically similar letters is worse than of those which are not similar. The effect was tested in multiple settings and seems to be highly robust. One potential explanation which was not extensively tested is that similar letters do not have discriminative retrieval cues, which leads to their poor recall. In this experiment, co-authored by Ana Avramović and Milica Šimonović and presented in Belgrade and Cambridge, the explanation was put to a direct test.

Developing and Validating the Masculinity Scale

Developed and validated scale which measures masculinity in collaboration with 4 colleagues of mine. During the first phase of this year-long project, a thorough literature review was conducted and specific subscales and items were constructed. Developed questionnaire was tested on a sample of 320 participants and results indicated which items should be excluded. The second phase consisted of checking construct, predictive, and diagnostic validity. For those purposes, a number of other scales were used as well, including those measuring agression, attitudes towards feminism, and HEXACO personality questionanire!
May 20, 2019

Translating and Validating General Risk Propensity Scale

Researchers interested in risk propensity traditionally considered risk taking as a domain-specific phenomenon. However, there is an evidence suggesting that risk taking could be considered as a general trait which could be measured by diffent techniques, among which are self-report questionnaires. Zhang and colleagues (2018) developed a short questionnaire for measuring general risk propensity. In this small project, the goal was to translate the scale into Serbian language, check its reliability, and explore its structural validity.
June 10, 2018

Exploring War Propaganda in Serbian Newspapers

Newspapers with fake news, spreading hatred, or even war propaganda is something that should come as no surprise for most people. However, most of these observations are just that - observations. For that reason, the goal of this small project was to collect data on war propaganda in Serbian daily newspapers in a random one-month period and to determine whether some newspapers write about defensive or offensive wars on their newspapers.
